# Wildfire Cinema Research

**Cinematic Representation of the Climate Crisis and Wildfires**
A corpus-based discourse analysis of film dialogue.

> Part of a PhD dissertation structured as a collection of papers,
> targeting *Environmental Communication* and *Science Communication* journals.

---

## About the project

This research examines how cinema constructs the climate crisis and wildfires
through dialogue, using corpus-assisted discourse studies (CADS) applied to
film and television subtitles. It is the first peer-reviewed, corpus-based
linguistic analysis of wildfire representation in cinema.

**Research gap:** Systematic searching across major academic databases located
no dedicated film-studies or literary-studies analysis of wildfire in cinema —
this project addresses that gap directly.

## Theoretical framework

Ten interlocking theoretical strands:

| Strand | Key scholars |
|--------|-------------|
| Framing theory | Entman (1993); Nisbet (2009) |
| Critical discourse analysis | Fairclough (1995); van Dijk (1993) |
| Narrative & genre theory | Green & Brock (2000) |
| Affect & risk communication | O'Neill & Nicholson-Cole (2009); Nabi (2003) |
| Construal level theory | Trope & Liberman (2010) |
| Agency & responsibility framing | Maniates (2001) |
| Public understanding of science | Wynne (1991); Bucchi (2008) |
| Cultivation theory | Gerbner & Gross (1976) |
| Cli-fi & climate cinema studies | Svoboda (2016); Salmose (2018) |
| Corpus-assisted discourse studies | Baker et al. (2008); Bednarek (2018) |
